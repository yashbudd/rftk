About the Event

A rivalry united by philanthropy

Run for the Kids is the annual philanthropy event of the Georgia Tech and University of Georgia chapters of Phi Gamma Delta (FIJI). Brothers from both campuses participate in a relay-style run, carrying the game ball nearly 70 miles between Atlanta and Athens in the week before Thanksgiving.

The run takes place ahead of Clean, Old-Fashioned Hate — one of college football's most storied rivalries — and every step is dedicated to supporting children and families across Georgia through CHOA.

  • Relay-style run across ~70 miles of Georgia
  • Game ball delivered ahead of the GT vs. UGA rivalry game
  • Fundraiser for Children's Healthcare of Atlanta
  • Open to financial, in-kind, and community partnerships
2026 Goal

Historically, the event has raised over $10,000 for CHOA. This year, we're expanding community and corporate support to make our greatest impact yet.

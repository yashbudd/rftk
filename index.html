About the Event

A rivalry united by philanthropy

Run for the Kids is the annual philanthropy event of the Georgia Tech and University of Georgia chapters of Phi Gamma Delta (FIJI). Brothers from both campuses participate in a relay-style run, carrying the game ball nearly 70 miles between Atlanta and Athens in the week before Thanksgiving.

The run takes place ahead of Clean, Old-Fashioned Hate, one of college football's most storied rivalries, and every step is dedicated to supporting children and families across Georgia through CHOA.

  • Relay-style run across ~70 miles of Georgia
  • Game ball delivered ahead of the GT vs. UGA rivalry game
  • Fundraiser for Children's Healthcare of Atlanta
  • Open to financial, in-kind, and community partnerships
2026 Goal

Historically, the event has raised over $10,000 for CHOA. This year, we're expanding community and corporate support to make our greatest impact yet.

Georgia Tech News

November 21, 2024

Running the Ball: Students Fundraise Ahead of Annual Rivalry Game

Georgia Tech highlighted Run for the Kids as brothers from the Georgia Tech and UGA FIJI chapters prepared to relay the game ball from Atlanta to Athens, raising funds for Children's Healthcare of Atlanta ahead of Clean, Old-Fashioned Hate.

"We go stadium to stadium and will bring three game balls with us. We have a lot of people who want to run this year after reviving the event last year."

Jack Ready, Georgia Tech FIJI · Georgia Tech News
Stadium to stadium Runners departed Bobby Dodd Stadium and handed the ball off to UGA brothers near Loganville.
Relay for CHOA Each runner covered roughly 2 miles in a relay format, with support vehicles and a police escort along the route.
Tradition revived After a pandemic hiatus, the event returned in 2023 and drew record interest from students wanting to run.
Destination: Sanford Stadium Three game balls were carried the full distance to Athens ahead of the annual rivalry matchup.
